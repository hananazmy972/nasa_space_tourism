Take a look at the solar system.

During the journey from Earth to Mars, you would have the opportunity to observe various objects and phenomena within the solar system. Here are some notable sights you might encounter: Moon, Planets, Asteroids, Comets, Interstellar Space, and Cosmic Background Radiation.

1: Moon: As the closest celestial body to Earth, the Moon would be visible during the initial stages of the journey. You might observe its surface features, such as craters, mountains, and maria (dark flat areas).
2: Planets: Depending on the alignment of the planets at the time of the mission, you might be able to observe other planets in the solar system. Planets like Venus, Jupiter, Saturn, and possibly others could be visible as bright points of light in the distance.
3: Asteroids: The asteroid belt, located between Mars and Jupiter, contains numerous rocky objects. Although the chances of directly encountering an asteroid are small, you might observe some of these objects from a distance through telescopes or cameras.
4: Comets: Comets are icy bodies that occasionally pass through the inner solar system. You might have the opportunity to observe its coma (atmosphere) and tail.
5: Interstellar Space: As the spacecraft ventures farther from the Sun, it will eventually reach interstellar space. This vast expanse between stars is relatively empty, but you might witness the occasional distant star or other astronomical phenomena.
6: Cosmic Background Radiation: Throughout the journey, you would continue to detect cosmic background radiation, which is the residual energy from the Big Bang. This radiation permeates the universe and appears as a faint, uniform glow.

Discovery of Korolev Crater on Mars

The crater is notable for containing a permanent ice deposit, which is a mixture of water ice and carbon dioxide ice (dry ice). The presence of water ice in the Korolev Crater is a valuable resource for human missions. You extract and process the ice to obtain drinking water, produce breathable oxygen, and even generate rocket propellant through methods such as electrolysis.

Korolev Crater is the largest skating rink in the solar system. The crater measures 51 miles (82 km) in diameter. The ice within this crater is a permanent feature, so you must be ready for the most epic game of hockey in the history of the solar system.

Exploring Olympus Mons on Mars

Olympus Mons is a prominent and fascinating feature on Mars. It is the largest volcano in the solar system and one of the most well-known features on Mars. It is a shield volcano, characterized by its broad, gently sloping shape and vast size. Olympus Mons stands at an impressive height of approximately 22 kilometers (13.6 miles) and has a diameter of about 624 kilometers (388 miles), making it nearly three times the height of Mount Everest and roughly the size of the state of Arizona.The surface of Olympus Mons exhibits a variety of geological features, including lava flows, calderas, and collapse pits. The volcano's shield shape is formed by the gradual accumulation of successive lava flows, creating a broad, gently sloping profile.
