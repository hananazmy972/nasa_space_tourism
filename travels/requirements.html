Personal Identification and Travel Documents

Visitors must carry their passports or identification documents required for international travel. Additionally, they should have copies of their travel itineraries, visas (if applicable), and any other necessary permits or authorizations.

Medical Clearance and Health Records:

Visitors should undergo a comprehensive medical examination to ensure they are fit for space travel. They must bring their medical clearance documents, including records of vaccinations and any necessary prescriptions or medications.

Appropriate Clothing and Footwear

Visitors should pack suitable clothing for the specific planetary conditions. This includes thermal undergarments, space-approved suits or pressure garments, outerwear, and comfortable footwear. The type of clothing will vary depending on the planet's temperature, atmospheric conditions, and gravity.

Personal Hygiene and Toiletries:

Visitors should bring personal hygiene items such as toothbrushes, toothpaste, soap, shampoo, and other essentials. Space-friendly and eco-friendly alternatives may be recommended to minimize waste and resource consumption during the tour.

Protective Gear and Equipment (provided)

Depending on the planet, visitors may require specific protective gear and equipment. This can include helmets, gloves, goggles, respirators, or specialized suits to protect against extreme temperatures, atmospheric pressures, or hazardous conditions.

Camera and Photography Equipmen

Space Photography is not just a regular photography service. We use high technology to ensure that our photos are of the highest quality and capture the beauty and uniqueness of each planet. Our photographers are equipped with special cameras that can adjust to different lighting conditions, gravity levels, and atmospheric pressures. They also have advanced drones that can fly around and take aerial shots of the tourists and the landscapes. We want to make your space trip unforgettable and share it with your loved ones.

Personal Entertainment and Electronic

Visitors may bring personal entertainment devices such as e-readers, tablets, or music players to enjoy during the journey. It is advisable to check compatibility with the spacecraft's power supply and ensure adherence to safety regulations regarding electronic devices.

Sense of Adventure and Curiosity

Last but not least, visitors should bring an open mind, a sense of adventure, and a curiosity to explore and learn about each planet and its unique characteristics. A positive attitude and willingness to adapt to the challenges and wonders of space travel will greatly enhance the tour experience.
